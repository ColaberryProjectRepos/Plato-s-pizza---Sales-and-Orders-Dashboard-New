Suggestions:

1. Provide discounts and free delivery

According to the data, Mondays are least popular for buying pizza among customers, showing decrease in sales by 29.01%. To make this situation better, the restaurant can offer regular discounts and provide free delivery option for orders that surpass a certain value. Also, it can create a regular custom that the customers will look forward to every week, something like - Magical Mondays, get 15% off on all orders.

2. Increase the presence on Digital Media Platforms
According to Datareportal, there are 4.48 billion social media users worldwide, which is almost 57% of the population. Of these users, 3 in 4 have used Facebook to make restaurant-related decisions, based on comments and images that have been shared by other users. Food and drink brands also dominate Twitter with a 32% share of tweets.
And the most Instagrammed food is pizza. So,  building a digital presence and leveraging the power of social media will help reach an even broader audience.

3. Customer Loyalty Programs
The data shows that the pizza orders are the highest on Wednesdays, with an increase of 24.95% from the orders on Tuesdays, which also suggests the possibility of more customers visiting the place. The restaurant can focus on the existing customers as a source of new revenue. Customer loyalty program can be designed by setting up a  referral program to get new customers by way of existing ones. Also, Incentivize existing customers to come at different times during the week. They should spend resources on the initiatives that will add the most revenue, not the most customers.

4. Upselling and Cross selling
The average order price for Mondays shows a drastic decrease of 3.3% compared to an increase of 3.09% for Sundays. Upselling and cross selling are both extremely successful marketing tactics that can be used by the restaurant to ensure that they get the maximum order value possible. Upselling is encouraging customers to increase the size of their meal or item from small to large. Cross selling is when the customer is placing an order, and the staff or website prompts them to add an extra item to their order, for example a drink or a dessert item. This will work to increase the average order value.
